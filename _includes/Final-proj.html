Fire battalions in San Francisco

Battalions are geographic divisions within the coverage area of the San Francisco Fire Department. Each battalion, consisting of several fire stations and firefighting personnel, is tasked with responding to emergency calls within its designated region. San Francisco is divided into 10 battalions, each identified by a unique code that begins with the letter 'B' followed by a two-digit number.

The plot displays the total number of calls for all 10 Battalions in San Francisco, categorized by year. It reveals a gradual increase in the number of calls over the years, with 2022 recording the highest number of calls to date.

To gain a deeper understanding of the data, we can concentrate on the year 2022, which recorded the highest number of calls. This information is vital for the fire department to effectively allocate resources and enhance preparedness for emergencies. It may also suggest a need for increased community education and awareness campaigns to prevent emergencies before they occur.

The average response time for each neighborhood in 2022.

Besides pinpointing the most frequent types of fire calls in San Francisco, it's crucial to assess the fire department's response time to these emergencies.

That’s why we’ve developed a plot illustrating the average response time for four different types of calls across various neighborhoods throughout the city.

As anticipated, the average response times vary considerably based on the type of call and the specific neighborhood. For instance, in December 2022, the average response time for the Noe Valley neighborhood is only 3 minutes and 25 seconds, while for the Golden Gate Park neighborhood, it is 6 minutes and 40 seconds.


But what does delay at response times means?

Average response times serve as a critical metric for emergency services like fire departments since they directly influence the outcomes of emergency incidents. In the context of fires, even a minute's delay in response time can potentially determine the disparity between life and death, as well as result in substantial property damage.

Consider this scenario: a mere few minutes of delay can escalate a minor fire into a major blaze, rendering it increasingly challenging and perilous for firefighters to control. Furthermore, each minute allocated to responding to a false alarm or non-emergency situation detracts from addressing potentially life-threatening emergencies.

Through comparing response times across various neighborhoods, we can pinpoint areas where the fire department might require additional resources or need to adapt their response strategies. Furthermore, this data enables us to enhance our comprehension of the correlation between call type, neighborhood, and response time.

This plot complements the map we previously generated, illustrating the distribution of these call types across various neighborhoods in San Francisco. By merging these two visualizations, we can attain a more holistic understanding of the fire department's management of emergencies across the city.
From both the map and the plot, it's apparent that there's a demand for a fire station in Golden Gate Park, particularly at the border between Inner Richmond and Haight Ashbury.

At what times are fire departments typically the busiest?

We've examined the hourly counts of fire incident calls received by the fire department. The data distinctly indicates that the majority of fire incidents happen during the day, with the highest volume of calls received between 9:00 to 21:00. Notably, there's a peak in fire calls between 17:00 to 19:00, signifying this as a notably busy period for the fire department.

Conversely, we observe that the lowest count of calls transpires between 3:00 to 6:00. This can be attributed to the fact that people are typically asleep during these hours, resulting in fewer activities that might lead to fires.

From these observations, it's evident that the fire department should concentrate its resources and efforts during the busiest hours of the day.

In doing so, they can enhance their service to the community and ensure swift responses to emergency calls.
